Research Overview

Research activities focus on Empirical Software Engineering and Information Systems, with particular emphasis on socio-technical systems and human aspects of software development. Current investigations examine online recruitment and selection practices, organizational decision-making processes, and the design of open research infrastructures that support transparency, data curation, and reproducibility.

Selected Publications

Accepted

Constructive Patterns for Human-Centered Tech Hiring

CHASE 2026 · Int. Conf. on the Human Aspects of Software Engineering

Guided by Applicant Attribution-Reaction Theory (AART), this study moves beyond diagnosing dysfunctions to design growth-oriented hiring. Based on 22 semi-structured interviews covering +470 R&S experiences, we identified 22 Constructive Patterns (CPs) that foster transparency, fairness, and humanized interactions in the software industry.

Under Peer Review

Anti-patterns in Online Recruitment and Selection Experienced by Early-Career Software Engineers

Empirical Software Engineering (EMSE) · Journal

Qualitative investigation identifying 25 distinct Anti-patterns (APs) in tech hiring. The study characterizes practices perceived as ineffective or unfair by novice SEs, providing actionable insights for organizations to improve candidate experience and mitigate perceptions of organizational illegitimacy.

An Overview of the dataWASHES Project and its Relation to University Extension at UFCA (Original title: "Uma Visão Geral sobre o Projeto dataWASHES e sua Relação com a Extensão Universitária na UFCA")

SEPEC 2024 · Extended Abstract

Presents dataWASHES, an open research infrastructure designed to provide programmatic access to the WASHES workshop proceedings. The work explores the intersection between Open Science and University Extension, demonstrating how technical APIs can democratize access to scientific data.
