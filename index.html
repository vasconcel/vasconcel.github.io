Research Overview

Research activities focus on Empirical Software Engineering and Information Systems, with particular emphasis on socio-technical systems and human aspects of software development. Current investigations examine online recruitment and selection practices, organizational decision-making processes, and the design of open research infrastructures that support transparency, data curation, and reproducibility.

Selected Publications

Accepted

Constructive Patterns for Human-Centered Tech Hiring

CHASE 2026 · International Conference on the Human Aspects of Software Engineering

This study investigates constructive socio-technical patterns in online recruitment and selection processes. Based on qualitative empirical data, the paper analyzes how organizational practices and candidate experiences interact, identifying recurring patterns that support transparency, fairness, and human-centered decision-making in software engineering hiring contexts.

Under Peer Review

Anti-patterns in Online Recruitment and Selection

Empirical Software Engineering (EMSE) · Journal

This paper reports a qualitative empirical investigation of anti-patterns perceived by early-career software engineers during online recruitment and selection processes. The study examines how specific practices negatively affect candidate experience, sense-making, and perceptions of organizational legitimacy.
