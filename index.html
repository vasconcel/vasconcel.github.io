Research Overview

Research focuses on Empirical Software Engineering and Information Systems, with particular emphasis on socio-technical systems and human aspects of software development. Current investigations examine online recruitment and selection practices, organizational decision-making processes, and the design of open research infrastructures that support transparency, data curation, and reproducibility.

Accepted

Constructive Patterns for Human-Centered Tech Hiring

CHASE 2026 · International Conference on the Human Aspects of Software Engineering

This study investigates constructive socio-technical patterns in technology-mediated recruitment and selection processes. Based on qualitative empirical data, the paper analyzes how organizational practices and candidate experiences interact, identifying recurring patterns that support transparency, fairness, and human-centered decision-making in software engineering hiring contexts.

Under Peer Review

Anti-patterns in Online Recruitment and Selection

Empirical Software Engineering (EMSE) · Journal

This paper reports a qualitative empirical investigation of anti-patterns perceived by early-career software engineers during online recruitment and selection processes. The study examines how specific practices negatively affect candidate experience, sense-making, and perceptions of organizational legitimacy, contributing to a deeper understanding of dysfunctional socio-technical dynamics in contemporary hiring systems.

Research Infrastructure & Open Science

dataWASHES: Open Research Data Infrastructure

Open-source Research Infrastructure · Data Curation

Open research infrastructure designed to support empirical and secondary studies on the Workshop on Social, Human, and Economic Aspects of Software (WASHES). The project addresses limitations of manual access to proceedings data by providing programmatic, curated, and reproducible access to papers, authors, and editions. dataWASHES contributes to open science practices by enabling data reuse, transparency, and methodological rigor in socio-technical software engineering research.
